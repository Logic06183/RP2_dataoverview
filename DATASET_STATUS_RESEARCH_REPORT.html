EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

This report provides a comprehensive technical assessment of the RP2 clinical dataset harmonization project, encompassing 18 clinical studies from Johannesburg, South Africa. The dataset represents a unique resource for climate-health analysis, combining HIV/AIDS clinical trial data, longitudinal health cohorts, and COVID-19 studies with standardized geographic and temporal metadata suitable for integration with climate exposure data.

Key Findings

Dataset Scale:

  • Total Records: 13,750 individual records
  • Unique Participants: 11,658 individuals
  • Temporal Coverage: 2002-2021 (19 years)
  • Geographic Focus: Johannesburg metropolitan area (100% geocoded)
  • Total Variables Harmonized: 116 standardized variables across 18 studies

Data Quality Metrics:

  • Overall Completeness: 89.2% (average across all studies)
  • Studies with >90% completeness: 10 of 18 (55.6%)
  • Studies with >95% completeness: 5 of 18 (27.8%)
  • Geocoding Success Rate: 100% (all records linked to precise coordinates)

Research Applications:

  • Climate-Health Analysis: READY - All studies geocoded and temporally referenced
  • Biomarker Availability: Comprehensive coverage of hematological, biochemical, and clinical markers
  • Longitudinal Capability: Multiple studies with repeated measures (784-2,550 records)
  • HIV-Climate Research: Excellent coverage of CD4, viral load, and treatment data

Readiness Assessment: The harmonized RP2 dataset is READY FOR CLIMATE-HEALTH ANALYSIS. Data quality is excellent, with comprehensive biomarker coverage, complete geocoding, and temporal metadata suitable for integration with ERA5 climate data. Recommended priority biomarkers include CD4 count (n=5,167), hemoglobin (n=5,598), glucose (n=2,750), and cholesterol (n=3,043).
