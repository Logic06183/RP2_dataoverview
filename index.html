Abstract

This report presents comprehensive data profiles for the quality-assured RP2 clinical dataset, comprising 11,114 records from 9,756 unique patients across 17 HIV-related studies conducted in Johannesburg, South Africa. The dataset has been fully validated using the ALCOA+ Data Integrity Framework, achieving 100% compliance across all biomarkers. All data undergo rigorous quality assurance including variable naming consistency, distribution analysis, outlier detection and removal, plausibility validation, cross-variable consistency checks, and unit standardization. The dataset integrates detailed demographic information, comprehensive clinical and laboratory biomarkers (HIV markers, hematology, biochemistry, vital signs), and daily climate exposure data with temperature lag periods, heat wave detection, and heat stress categorization. This documentation provides detailed study profiles and comprehensive QA reports for appropriate dataset utilization in climate-health research.

The RP2 clinical dataset integrates participant-level data from 17 independent HIV-related clinical studies conducted through various research institutions in Johannesburg. This quality-assured dataset has undergone comprehensive validation using the ALCOA+ Data Integrity Framework, including DACS definition, variable naming consistency, distribution analysis, outlier detection and removal, plausibility validation, cross-variable consistency checks, unit conversions, and complete data traceability. The dataset combines comprehensive clinical and laboratory measurements with daily climate exposure data linked to each participant's visit date. Data profiles are organized into three sections: Demographics (age, sex, race, study information), Health & Biomarkers (vital signs, anthropometry, hematology, biochemistry, HIV markers), and Climate (temperature metrics, heat waves, heat stress categories).

4.Dataset Contents

4.1 Demographics & Visit Information

Patient ID, age, sex, race, city, visit dates, year, month, season, latitude, and longitude.

4.2 Health & Biomarkers

HIV Markers: HIV status, ART status, viral suppression status, CD4 count, viral load (with undetectable flag and log10 transform)
Hematology: Hemoglobin, hematocrit, RBC count, WBC count, platelet count, MCV, MCH, MCHC, RDW, differential counts (neutrophils, lymphocytes, monocytes, eosinophils, basophils)
Biochemistry: Fasting glucose, total cholesterol, HDL, LDL, triglycerides, creatinine, creatinine clearance, BUN, ALT, AST, alkaline phosphatase, total bilirubin, albumin, total protein, sodium, potassium, calcium
Vital Signs: Systolic BP, diastolic BP, heart rate, temperature, respiratory rate, oxygen saturation
Anthropometrics: Height, weight, BMI, waist circumference

4.3 Climate Exposures

Temperature Metrics: Daily mean, maximum, minimum, and range
Lag Periods: Temperature values at 1, 3, 7, 14, 21, and 30 days prior to visit
Variability: 7-day and 30-day temperature variability
Derived Indices: Apparent temperature, temperature anomaly, heat wave indicator (binary), heat stress category (categorical)
All climate data linked by visit date to enable exposure-outcome analysis.
