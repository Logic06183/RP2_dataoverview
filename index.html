Abstract

This report presents comprehensive data profiles for the RP2 clinical dataset, comprising 11,026 patient records across 17 HIV-related studies conducted in Johannesburg, South Africa. The dataset includes detailed demographic information, comprehensive clinical and laboratory biomarkers, and integrated daily climate exposure data. Each study profile provides exploratory data analysis organized into three sections: Demographics, Health & Biomarkers, and Climate exposures. Ten production-ready studies (9,987 participants, 90.6% of dataset) are available for analysis. This documentation provides detailed study profiles and guidance for appropriate dataset utilization in climate-health research and epidemiological analysis.

The RP2 clinical dataset integrates participant-level data from 17 independent HIV-related clinical studies conducted through various research institutions in Johannesburg. The dataset combines comprehensive clinical and laboratory measurements with daily climate exposure data linked to each participant's visit date. Data profiles are organized into three sections: Demographics (age, sex, race, study information), Health & Biomarkers (vital signs, anthropometry, hematology, biochemistry, HIV markers), and Climate (temperature, heat stress indices, seasonal factors).

3.Dataset Contents

3.1 Demographics

Age at enrollment, sex, race, study enrollment and visit dates, study arm assignments, and antiretroviral therapy status.

3.2 Health & Biomarkers

Anthropometrics: BMI, weight, height, waist/hip circumference
Vital Signs: Blood pressure, heart rate, temperature, oxygen saturation
HIV Markers: CD4 count, viral load, CD4/CD8 ratio
Hematology: Complete blood count, differential counts
Biochemistry: Liver function (ALT, AST, bilirubin, albumin), renal function (creatinine, eGFR), electrolytes, lipid profile, glucose metabolism
Other: Inflammatory markers, coagulation studies

3.3 Climate Exposures

Daily temperature (mean, maximum, minimum), temperature anomalies, heat day indicators (90th and 95th percentiles), heat stress index, humidity, precipitation, and seasonal classifications. Climate data are linked to each participant's visit date.
