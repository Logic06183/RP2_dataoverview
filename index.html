The RP2 clinical dataset integrates participant-level data from 13 HIV-related clinical studies conducted through various research institutions in Johannesburg, South Africa. This enhanced release features comprehensive YData profiling with complete data lineage traceability for all 73,125 records from 12,804 unique patients. Each study profile includes detailed statistical analysis, variable distributions, missing data patterns, correlation analysis, and full data lineage documentation showing source files, column mappings, units, and transformations. The dataset has undergone rigorous quality assurance using the ALCOA+ Data Integrity Framework, including DACS definition, variable naming consistency, distribution analysis, outlier detection and removal, plausibility validation, cross-variable consistency checks, unit conversions, and complete data traceability. The dataset combines comprehensive clinical and laboratory measurements (96 variables) with daily climate exposure data linked to each participant's visit date. Data profiles are organized into three sections: Demographics (age, sex, race, study information), Health & Biomarkers (vital signs, anthropometry, body composition, hematology, biochemistry, HIV markers), and Climate (temperature metrics, heat waves, heat stress categories).

4.Dataset Contents

4.1 Demographics & Visit Information

Patient ID, age, sex, race, city, visit dates, year, month, season, latitude, and longitude.

4.2 Health & Biomarkers

HIV Markers: HIV status, ART status, viral suppression status, CD4 count, viral load (with undetectable flag and log10 transform)
Hematology: Hemoglobin, hematocrit, RBC count, WBC count, platelet count, MCV, MCH, MCHC, RDW, differential counts (neutrophils, lymphocytes, monocytes, eosinophils, basophils)
Biochemistry: Fasting glucose, total cholesterol, HDL, LDL, triglycerides, creatinine, creatinine clearance, BUN, ALT, AST, alkaline phosphatase, total bilirubin, albumin, total protein, sodium, potassium, calcium
Vital Signs: Systolic BP, diastolic BP, heart rate, temperature, respiratory rate, oxygen saturation
Anthropometrics: Height, weight, BMI, waist circumference

4.3 Climate Exposures

Temperature Metrics: Daily mean, maximum, minimum, and range
Lag Periods: Temperature values at 1, 3, 7, 14, 21, and 30 days prior to visit
Variability: 7-day and 30-day temperature variability
Derived Indices: Apparent temperature, temperature anomaly, heat wave indicator (binary), heat stress category (categorical)
All climate data linked by visit date to enable exposure-outcome analysis.
